Overview

A dialysis department is a specialized medical facility or unit within a hospital that provides dialysis treatment for patients with kidney failure. Dialysis is a medical procedure that involves removing waste and excess fluids from the blood when the kidneys are no longer able to do so on their own. This can be done using either hemodialysis, which involves using a machine to filter the blood, or peritoneal dialysis, which involves using the lining of the abdomen to filter the blood. Dialysis departments typically have specially trained healthcare professionals, including nurses, technicians, and nephrologists, who oversee the treatment of patients and ensure their safety and comfort during the procedure. The department may also provide other related services such as education and counseling for patients and their families.

Diseases of dialysis

  • End-stage renal disease (ESRD)
  • Chronic kidney disease (CKD)
  • Acute kidney injury (AKI)
  • Glomerulonephritis
  • Polycystic kidney disease (PKD)
  • Diabetic nephropathy
  • Lupus nephritis
  • Renal artery stenosis
  • Congenital abnormalities of the kidneys

    The main types of dialysis treatment include:

    Hemodialysis: The most common form of dialysis, hemodialysis uses a machine to filter blood outside of the body, using a dialyzer and a circuit of tubes to remove waste and excess fluid.
    Peritoneal dialysis: This type of dialysis uses the lining of the abdominal cavity as a filter. A catheter is inserted into the abdominal cavity to allow a special solution to flow in and out, filtering the blood.
    Continuous ambulatory peritoneal dialysis (CAPD): This is a type of peritoneal dialysis that can be performed at home. The patient performs the procedure themselves, usually four times a day.
    Continuous cycling peritoneal dialysis (CCPD): Another type of peritoneal dialysis, this is performed at night while the patient sleeps, with the help of a machine.
    The choice of dialysis treatment will depend on the individual patient's needs and medical history, as well as their lifestyle and preferences. It is important to discuss the different options with a healthcare professional to determine the best course of treatment.
